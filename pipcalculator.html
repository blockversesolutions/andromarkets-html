How to calculate pip value using the calculator

To find out the pip value of a certain currency pair or another asset, enter your order details in the calculator.

  • Symbol is the asset you are planning to trade.

  • Account currency is the deposit currency of your Octa trading account.

  • Pip amount is the expected price movement in pips.

  • Volume, lots is your position size in lots. One standard lot is 100,000 units of the base currency (listed first in a pair).

  • Ask price is the current price of your asset on the market. You can freely change it.

The pip calculator uses the current Ask price to determine the value of one pip (shown under Pip value) in case the price moves by the number of pips indicated in the Pip amount field.

Example: how to calculate pips in Forex trading

  • Let us say you want to buy a standard lot of EURGBP, or 100,000 EUR. The current exchange rate for this pair is 0.84730, and you expect it to move 5 pips up—to 0.84780. Each pip is worth 10 GBP for this order, so its expected value change is 50 GBP. The exchange rate for the GBPUSD pair is 1.29820 at the moment, so 1 GBP is worth 1.29820 USD. That means each pip equals 12.9820 USD—that is our Pip value—and your expected profit of 50 GBP equals 64.9100 USD.

How to use the pip calculator for trading

  • Using the Forex pip calculator to calculate pip value and potential profit helps you understand possible risks and opportunities in clear monetary terms. Depending on your order size, a pip can amount to $1 as well as $1,000, so you should always consider pip value when deciding whether an order is worth the risk. This pip calculator is a great tool for determining Stop Loss and Take Profit levels. But keep in mind that pip value is different for every currency pair.

FAQ

One pip is the smallest unit of price change in Forex. For most trading instruments, a pip refers to the fourth decimal point, or 0.0001. However, there are assets with different pip meanings:

  • 3-digit currency pairs and XAGUSD—the 2nd decimal (0.01)

  • XAUUSD, XBRUSD, XTIUSD, and indices (except JPN225)—the 1st decimal (0.1).
